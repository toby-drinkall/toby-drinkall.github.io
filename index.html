About

In 2025 I graduated Oxford (MS in Data Science - Distinction). Alongside the taught programme I built simulations in Python to stress-test AI agents as decision-makers, leading work on some of the first benchmarks for models’ military targeting behaviour (backed by Schmidt Futures and UC Berkeley).

Previously, I built a company helping boutique consultancies with their AI transformation, judged a Sequoia-backed hackathon in New York, and delivered a course for BlueDot Impact on AI Existential Risk & Governance. I initially studied Theology and Religion at Oxford (BA - First Class with Honours) - a degree I love(d) that still shapes how I think and build – now, I focus on creating systems that support decisions that matter.

I hold an offer from Palantir, who are supporting my O-1A “extraordinary ability” visa to move to the Bay Area in 2026. For more info, check out my projects, thoughts, and references below.
