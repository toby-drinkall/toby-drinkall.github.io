About

In 2025 I graduated Oxford (MS in Data Science - Distinction). Alongside the taught programme I built simulations in Python to stress-test AI agents as decision-makers, leading work on some of the first benchmarks for models’ military targeting behaviour (backed by Schmidt Futures and UC Berkeley).

Previously, I built a company helping boutique consultancies with their AI transformation, judged a Sequoia-backed hackathon in New York, and delivered a course for BlueDot Impact on AI Existential Risk & Governance. I initially studied Theology and Religion at Oxford (BA - First Class with Honours) - a degree I love(d) that still shapes how I think and build – now, I focus on creating systems that support decisions that matter.

I hold an offer from Palantir, who are supporting my O-1A “extraordinary ability” visa to move to the Bay Area in 2026. For more info, check out my projects, thoughts, and references below.

Projects

  • [AI Evaluation] Red Lines and Grey Zones in the Fog of War: Benchmarking Targeting Decisions

    Oxford MS Thesis

    Built a multi-agent wargame in Python to benchmark targeting decisions made by military LLM agents. Backed by Oxford, Schmidt Futures & UC Berkeley. Presented at Hoover, Oxford, DC, and the Air University Innovation Accelerator (AUiX); invited by NATO IST to present.

  • [Cryptography] Delegated Doctrine: Reversing Subversive Tendencies of Military AI Decision-Support Systems

    Oxford Internet Institute

    ~2025

    Analysed how AI-enabled decision-support systems can subtly reshape doctrine and control, and proposed design patterns to preserve meaningful human control, accountability, and transparency.

  • [Strategy] Strategic Creativity in Human–AI Collaboration

    Oxford Internet Institute · Alan Turing Institute · UK Ministry of Defence

    Explored how AI agent systems influence strategic reasoning in adversarial settings, and how human–AI teams can be structured to preserve creativity, normative alignment, and robust oversight. Helped formalise “agentic AI” in defence contexts.
